About Kishtwar

Kishtwar is a town, municipality and administrative headquarter of the Kishtwar district in the Indian-administered union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. The district was carved out of the Doda district in 2007, and is located in the Chenab Valley of the Jammu division. Kishtwar is well-connected by roads to other places in Jammu and Kashmir and rest of India, as several highways and roads pass through Kishtwar including NH 244.

About Machail Mata

The Machail Mata Yatra is an annual Hindu pilgrimage to the remote Shri Chandi Mata temple in the Paddar Valley of Kishtwar District, Jammu & Kashmir. This challenging spiritual journey involves a trek through scenic, mountainous terrain to reach the high-altitude shrine, attracting thousands of devotees annually. The Yatra starts from Bhaderwah or Jammu, with the final roadhead being Gulabgarh, from where pilgrims undertake the 30-32 km trek on foot, often taking two days.

About Sarthal Mata

A Hindu shrine, Sarthal Mata is famous for the annual pilgrimage known as Sarthal Yatra. The idol, considered as re-incarnation of Goddess Durga, was originally carved from stones by locals during the Raja Agar Dev of the Kishtwar period and later, renovated by Maharaja Hari Singh in 1936. It is situated at approximately 6000 feet and usually covered with snow during winters

Kishtwar Jammu and Kashmir Ziyarat Shareef Hazrat Sakhi zein ud din wali(ra) . Bhandarkoot kishtwar.

The Bhandarkoot Ziyarat is a revered spiritual site in Kishtwar, Jammu and Kashmir, known for its association with Hazrat Sheikh Zain-ud-din Rishi and its location at the confluence of the Marusudar and Chenab rivers. While the Ziyarat itself doesn't have an independent history distinct from the saint, it is a significant place of worship for both Muslims and Hindus, who perform rituals and offer prayers seeking blessings. The site also holds mythological significance, as it's believed to be the place where the mythological figure Jatayu was cremated.

Kishtwar Ganga mata mandir#video Bhandarkoot Kishtwar

The history of Bhandarkoot Ganga Mata Mandir in Kishtwar is rooted in local legends, particularly one from the Ramayana era, stating that it was the place where Lord Rama performed the goudan (cow donation) for Jatayu and invoked Ganga Mata at the confluence of the Chenab (Chandrabhaga) and Marivsodar rivers. The temple is believed to house the goddess herself, making it a sacred pilgrimage site and a place of interfaith harmony, particularly during the annual Baisakhi festival.

Kishtwar Saffron

Kishtwar saffron from Jammu and Kashmir's Kishtwar district is a high-quality, prized spice known for its exceptional color, flavor, and aroma, receiving a Geographical Indication (GI) tag. It is cultivated in the district's mountainous areas, with the Mandal region being a key production zone, and is distinguished by its unique biochemical composition, making it superior to Pampore saffron. Kishtwar saffron is used in cooking, traditional medicine, and cultural practices and is highly valued for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Neelam (blue sapphire) Stone

Kishtwar, in Jammu and Kashmir, India, is renowned for its Neelam (blue sapphire) stones, particularly those found in the Padder Valley. These sapphires are highly prized for their velvety texture and exceptional clarity, often described as cornflower blue due to the presence of iron and titanium. The discovery of these stones in the late 1800s led to a boom in the region, though mining has since become rare. The blue sapphires are found in the Zanskar Range of the Himalayas, specifically in the Padder Valley of the Kishtwar district. Kashmiri Neelam is known for its unique velvety texture, caused by the presence of parallel silk lines within the stone. The cornflower blue color is a hallmark, making it a desirable gemstone.

Kishtwar Gucchi

"Kishtwar Gucchi" refers to high-quality, natural saffron (kesar) from the Kishtwar Valley in Jammu and Kashmir. This premium saffron is known for its distinctive aroma, rich flavor, and is used in culinary dishes, teas, and beauty treatments. It is a specialty of the region and is collected from wild sources. The term "Gucchi" refers to a rare, handpicked variety of saffron known for its excellent quality. The saffron is sourced from the secluded Kishtwar region of Jammu and Kashmir, a location renowned for producing high-grade saffron. It consists of natural, dried saffron strands, often described as authentic, rare, and Grade A1 quality. It is used to enhance the flavor of various dishes like pulao, biryani, and paella, as well as in teas.

Chenab River(Chander Bhaga)

The Chenab River flows through the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir, shaping the region's topography and forming the Chenab Valley in its southern areas. It is a major artery of the Chenab River basin, which also covers parts of Himachal Pradesh, and is crucial for several significant hydroelectric projects, including the Kwar Hydro Electric Project located on the river in Kishtwar district. The Chenab River flows through Kishtwar, creating a fertile Chenab Valley in the southern part of the district. Hydroelectric Projects: The district is home to several significant hydroelectric projects, including the Kwar project, which are a crucial part of India's energy security and utilize the river's water. Marusudar River: A tributary of the Chenab, the Marusudar River, also flows through Kishtwar and is the site of the proposed Bursar Hydroelectric project.
